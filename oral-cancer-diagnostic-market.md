<p><p>The oral cancer diagnostic market includes various imaging and testing techniques used for detection and evaluation. Computed Tomography (CT) scans provide detailed images of the mouth and surrounding tissues. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) offers high-resolution views of soft tissues. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) detects metabolic activity in cancer cells. Ultrasonography uses sound waves for real-time imaging. Biopsy involves tissue sampling for histological analysis. X-ray provides basic images of structures. Endoscopy allows direct visualization of oral cavities, while Fluorescence Imaging enhances lesion detection. Other methods may include advanced imaging technologies and diagnostic platforms.</p></p>
