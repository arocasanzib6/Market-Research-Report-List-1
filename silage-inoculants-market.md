<p><p>Silage inoculants are additives used to enhance the fermentation process of silage, improving its preservation and quality. The market is primarily divided into two types: wet inoculants and dry inoculants. Wet inoculants are liquid formulations, often easier to apply and typically contain live bacteria, providing immediate fermentation benefits. Dry inoculants, however, are powdered and often include concentrated strains of bacteria, offering a longer shelf life and easier handling. Both types aim to boost nutrient retention and overall silage quality.</p></p>
