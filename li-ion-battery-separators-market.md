<p><p>The Li-Ion battery separators market encompasses two primary manufacturing methods: wet and dry methods. The wet method involves dissolving polymer materials in a solvent, creating a thin, porous film through casting or extrusion, which enhances ionic conductivity and mechanical strength. Conversely, the dry method utilizes non-solvent techniques, such as calendaring or extrusion, to produce separators with distinct characteristics, often resulting in lower production costs and improved thermal stability. Both methods cater to diverse application requirements in energy storage.</p></p>
